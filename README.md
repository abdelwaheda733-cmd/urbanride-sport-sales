This dashboard was built بالكامل باستخدام Excel, leveraging Power Query for data cleaning and transformation, and Pivot Tables & Pivot Charts for analysis and visualization. The layout was designed to be clear, interactive, and easy to interpret.
In the data preparation phase, I used Power Query to remove duplicates and null values, ensure correct data types (dates, numbers, text), and create calculated columns where needed. The cleaned data was then loaded into structured tables for analysis.
From the yearly sales analysis, 2016 represents about 55% of total sales compared to 44% in 2015, indicating clear year-over-year growth.
Looking at sales by gender, males account for 51% of total sales, while females represent 49%, showing a nearly balanced distribution.
By product category:
Bikes contribute  51% of total sales,
Accessories  33%,
Clothing  15%.
This shows that Bikes are the primary revenue driver.
When comparing costs by category, Bikes also have the highest cost share at 55%, which aligns with their high sales volume. This opens the door for deeper profitability analysis.
For customer age analysis, the 26–31 age group generates the highest sales, making them a key target segment for marketing strategies.
Geographically, the United States accounts for about 46% of total sales and 48% of total costs, making it the strongest market overall.
Conclusion:
There is clear growth from 2015 to 2016.
Bikes are the top-performing product category.
The U.S. is the primary market.
Customers aged 26–31 are the most valuable segment.
Strategically, the focus should be on strengthening bike sales, targeting the most active age group, and improving profitability in high-performing markets.
